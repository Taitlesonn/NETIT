Playing the Shell Game

You need a way to tell the computer what you want it to do. In Linux, one of 
the ways to communicate with the computer is through something called the 
shell. A 

shell

 isn’t a graphical thing; it’s the sum total of the commands and 

syntax you have available to you to tell the operating system what to do so 
you can do your work.

The shell environment is rather dull and boring by graphical desktop stan-
dards. When you start the shell, all you see is a plain window with a short 
prompt (such as a 

$

) followed by a blinking cursor that awaits your keyboard 

entry. (Later in this section, we show you a couple of methods for accessing 
the shell.)

The default shell used in Linux is the 

bash

 shell. This work environment is 

based on the original UNIX shell, which is called the 

Bourne shell

 and is also 

referred to as 

sh

. The term 

bash

 stands for the 

B

ourne 

a

gain 

sh

ell. The 

bash

 

shell comes with most Linux distributions.
