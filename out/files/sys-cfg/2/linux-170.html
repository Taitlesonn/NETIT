he 

Internet

 is a vast network of computers that spans the globe. Many 

different types of computers and operating systems work together to 

allow you access to information across this network. Linux, along with the 
other related UNIX operating systems, has long supported and worked with 
the Internet. Practically all the different services available on the Internet are 
available from your Linux desktop.

When you install Linux (see Chapter 3), you get one or more Web browsers, 
e-mail programs, and instant-messaging tools placed on your new system. In this 
chapter, we introduce you to some of those tools; you can use them to access 
different services on the Internet — such as Web sites, e-mail, newsgroups, and 
FTP — after your Internet connection is configured (see Chapter 8).
